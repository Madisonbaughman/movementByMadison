Both! We all have physical goals, and some may struggle more than others. With my background as an OT, I can address child delays having had experience working in outpatient pediatric settings and summer programs. But adults have movement and strength goals too!! Sometimes we neglect these and end up uncomfortable in our bodies or not able to perform to the degree we would like to be. Rehabbing clients after injury back to health and personally training those who want to achieve stronger, healthier bodies offers a “lifespan” approach which I am happy to be able to provide as a therapist and trainer.
I see kids with motor or coordination difficulties as well kids that may lack strength. This includes gross and fine motor issues. Some may have mild issues (“My child seems a little ‘clumsy.’”) and some have more severe delays. I also see some younger children that might be a little slower to meet their milestones (rolling over, crawling etc).
